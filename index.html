About Me

I'm a PhD candidate in the Astronomy Department at University of Maryland - College Park. Broadly, my research seeks to answer questions about why our Universe resembles the background video above, with a particular interest in dark matter and dark energy! I include introductions to these topics and how my research fits into them below.

I currently work with Prof. Benedikt Diemer researching a better framework to study dark matter in universe-scale simulations. I'm also interested in work drawing maps of the universe and galaxy formation and evolution. In the past, I have worked with Professors Peter Timbie and Elena D'Onghia at the University of Wisconsin - Madison on those topics. Outside of research, I invest my time building support networks for neurodivergent individuals and improving science communication. If you want to get to know me a little better, I include some personal interests in the About Me page.

Research

Mapping the Universe

The mysterious force that drives the accelerating expansion of the Universe known as dark energy remains outside our current understanding of physics. Maps of the universe allows us to track and understand dark energy's behavior, bringing us closer to solving this puzzle.

  • Learn More

Dark Matter

Structures called "dark matter halos" are the Universe's building blocks. Cosmologists used them to construct theoretical frameworks for how the Cosmic Web formed and developed and to measure dark matter's properties.

  • Learn More

Galaxy Evolution

As dark matter collapses into halos, it guides the formation and evolution of embedded galaxies. As a result, the properties of galaxies and their host dark matter halos are inextricably tied.
