About Me

I'm a third-year graduate student in the Astronomy Department at University of Maryland - College Park. I currently work with Prof. Benedikt Diemer researching a better framework to study dark matter in universe-scale simulations. I'm also interested in 21cm intensity mapping and large-scale structure. Outside of research, I'm particularly passionate about improving public speaking and verbal communication skills in science.

More details on my projects are in the sections below! If you want to get to know me a little better, I include some personal interests and hobbies in the About Me page.

Research

Hydrogen and Galaxies

Future experiments intend to use hydrogen to map the large-scale structure of the universe and constrain the behavior of dark energy. We use computer simulations to understand what how the processes within galaxies may complicate this future technique. In particular, we focus on how the color of a galaxy, which reflects its state of star formation, influences the universal distribution of hydrogen.
