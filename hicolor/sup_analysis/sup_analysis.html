Comparing color to star formation activity

A galaxy that qualifies as "red" is not necessarily always quiescent. We examine how different the color distribution is from the star-formation rate distribution here.

evolution of HI auto power

The redshift evolution of the HI auto power spectrum, shown as a ratio over the z = 0 result. The contours are excluded for ease of reading. The auto power increases between redshifts 1 and 0.5, but decreases significantly between 0 and 0.5. This highlights that quenching galaxies play a large role in the evolution of structure in the HI distribution.

evolution of galaxy auto powers

The redshift evolution of the blue (left), red (center), and all (right) galaxy auto powers. The galaxy auto power increases with time, which is the expected evolution for power spectra. However, the both of the colored auto powers decrease with time between the latter two snapshots. This is also seen in the cross-powers, but this removes the influence of the relationship between galaxy color and HI, at least directly.
